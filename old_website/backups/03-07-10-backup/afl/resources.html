Internet Resources for Women in the Sciences

  • TAP: The Ada Project
    A clearinghouse for information and resources relating to women and computing. Includes fellowship and grant information, job openings, and much more of interest to both men and women. A must-visit for all Ph.D. students.

  • Special Conference on Women in Engineering and Science - Bridging the Gender Gap in Engineering and Science: the Challenge of Institutional Change

  • 1993-94 Taulbee Survey Available - Including Statistics on Women in CS
    The annual Taulbee survey, conducted by the Computing Research Association, is the primary source of information on North American faculty and Ph.D. production in computer science and computer engineering.

  • PipeLINK Home Page
    Connecting Women Across the C.S. Pipeline. Lists information for undergraduate and graduate women in Computer Science. Contains information on summer programs for high schools students at RPI.

  • Women and Computer Science
    A collection on of articles on under representations of women in computer science.

  • Stanford University Women in Computer Science

  • University of Wisconsin Women in Computer Science Women in Computer Sciences (WICS) is an informal group of all female members of the Computer Sciences Department here at UW-Madison. This includes students (both graduate and undergraduate), faculty, and research/teaching staff.

  • Society of Women Engineers
    The Society of Women Engineers (SWE) stimulates women to achieve full potential in careers as engineers and leaders, expands the image of the engineering profession as a positive force in improving the quality of life, and demonstrates the value of diversity.

  • Association for Women in Science
    AWIS is an organization dedicated to increasing the educational and employment opportunities for both girls and women in all fields of science.

  • Women in Engineering Industry
    A speech given by Kaitlin Duck Sherwood on women working in engineering industry.

  • The Archives of Women in Science and Engineering
    The Iowa State University Library inaugurated its Archives of Women in Science and Engineering in April 1994. This new archive was born from the need to document the history of women in science and engineering--their social history as well as the story of their scientific achievements.

  • Caltech Women's Center
    The Caltech Women's Center was founded in 1993 to work for the advancement of women in science and engineering. The Center works to supports the central mission of the California Institute of Technology, which is to promote the education and development of all scientists and engineers.

  • Women in Science and Engineering
    The mission of the Women in Science and Engineering (WISE) Program is to expand and improve the educational and professional opportunities or women in all fields of science and technology by facilitating institutional and social change.

  • University of Calgary Women in Science and Engineering

  • Women in Math (WIM)
    Women in Math (WIM) is an organization at the University of Maryland College Park (UMCP). WIM is dedicated to serving the needs of women mathematicians at UMCP.

  • Info about Take Our Daughters to Work Day
    Join millions of girls and adults around the country as they go to work together on April 27, 1995 for Take Our Daughters to Work Day! Take Our Daughters to Work Day is a national program sponsored by the Ms. Foundation. It is a special day dedicated to girls ages 9-15 in hopes that they will see potential for themselves in the workplace by spending one day at work with a parent, grandparent, sibling, neighbor, family friend, or someone simply interested in giving them an opportunity.
