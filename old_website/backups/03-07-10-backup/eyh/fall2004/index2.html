You Can Program

Today you are going to learn how to program! There are many programming tools out there; perhaps you have heard of some: Java, Pascal, C++, Pizza, Perl, Logo,... A programming language lets you talk with the computer. In many ways it is just like sending an e-mail to one of your friends. In an e-mail, you type your message in a language (perhaps english or spanish) that both you and your friend understand. When you talk with the computer, you need to use a language that the computer can understand. To learn these computer words for Logo, go to http://www.cs.wisc.edu/~deppeler/eyh/f04/LOGO_syntax.txt

Moving Turtles

Logo is a great programming language to introduce you to the wonderful world of programming! In the Logo world you will direct a turtle to move as you wish. We will be following the instructions found at the tutorial for Saturday's class. If you would like to download your own copy of Logo to use at home, you need to go to http://library.thinkquest.org/18446/eindex.shtml and click on the Download link. If you would like to program online (no downloading necessary), just go to the same site above and click on Program.

If you would like to direct more than one turtle, then StarLogo is the programming language for you. To install StarLogo on your own computer, you need to go to http://education.mit.edu/starlogo/ and click on "Download." Now just follow the instructions and you will have your own copy of StarLogo to use at home.
